Birth Information

Atal Bihari Vajpayee was born on December 25, 1924, in Gwalior, Madhya Pradesh, India.

History

Atal Bihari Vajpayee was a Indian politician who served as the 10th Prime Minister of India, serving three terms in office: first for a term of 13 days in 1996, then for a period of 13 months from 1998-1999, and finally, for a full five-year term from 1999 to 2004.

Vajpayee was a member of the Bharatiya Jana Sangh political party, which later became the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). He was known for his progressive policies and initiatives, such as the Golden Quadrilateral project, which aimed to connect India's major cities through highways, and the Pokhran nuclear tests, which established India as a nuclear power.

Vajpayee was a charismatic and visionary leader who was widely respected across political and ideological lines. He passed away on August 16, 2018, leaving behind a lasting legacy in Indian politics and society.

About Atal Bihari Vajpayee

Atal Bihari Vajpayee was a former Prime Minister of India and a veteran politician. He was known for his visionary leadership and for his efforts to improve India's infrastructure and economy. He was also a poet and a great orator.

Achievements

  • Became the 10th Prime Minister of India in 1996
  • Pushed for the development of India's infrastructure, including the Golden Quadrilateral highway project and the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana
  • Successfully conducted India's nuclear tests in 1998
  • Initiated peace talks with Pakistan and signed the Lahore Declaration in 1999

Quotes

  • "People who will not fight eventually will be eaten up by those who will fight."
  • "Peace is not just the absence of conflict. It is a positive concept of mutual support, understanding and respect."
  • "The only way to do great work is to love what you do."
